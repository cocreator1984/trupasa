Overview

Trupasa is a sovereign financial infrastructure platform that enables governments to generate annual income from non-inflationary digital reserves and invest the yield (interest earnings) from U.S. Treasury Bills into public development projects. The Trupasa landing page will serve as the public face of this platform, explaining its core concept and benefits in a clear, credible manner. It must appeal to government officials and serious institutional investors by conveying trust, stability, and professionalism. This document outlines the requirements and content for the Trupasa landing page, which will be implemented via Codex CLI.

Target Audience

The primary audience for the Trupasa landing page is government officials and institutional investors. This includes individuals such as:

Finance Ministers, Central Bank Governors, and Treasury Officials – policymakers who would be interested in new tools for national finance.

Sovereign Wealth Fund Managers and Economists – experts looking for innovative yet safe ways to grow national reserves.

Development Bank and NGO Leaders – those involved in funding public projects who might support or partner with such initiatives.

Institutional Investors \& Advisors – large-scale investors or financial advisors who might invest in or endorse Trupasa’s model (or participate if there’s an investment component).

These readers are professional, busy, and likely skeptical. They will look for evidence of credibility (backing, compliance, real-world results) and clear explanations of how Trupasa works.

Provide enough detail to satisfy more technical readers (e.g. mention of U.S. Treasury Bills, yields, compliance frameworks), but structure it so that a quick skim still delivers the key message (using headlines, bullet points, and summary statements).

Accessibility: Since government officials often require sites to meet accessibility standards, implement WCAG-compliant practices: sufficient color contrast, ability to navigate via keyboard, screen-reader friendly labels, etc. This aligns with building trust and professionalism.

Technical \& Functional Requirements

In addition to design and content, the implementation must meet several technical requirements to ensure the page is effective and maintainable:

SEO Best Practices: As mentioned, the page should include:

A unique Title tag (e.g., <title>Trupasa – Sovereign Digital Reserves Platform</title>).

Meta Keywords are optional (search engines give them little weight now), but could include keywords like "sovereign digital currency, government reserves platform, stablecoin, U.S. Treasury yield, public infrastructure financing". Use sparingly and only if relevant.

Create a robots.txt allowing search engines to crawl the site, and a simple sitemap.xml if there are multiple pages (for now, mainly relevant if blog posts are separate pages).

SEO \& Analytics Considerations

(Some of this reiterates earlier points in context, but collected here for completeness for the implementation team.)

Page Title \& Metadata: As mentioned, use a concise yet keyword-rich title. For example:

<title>Trupasa – Sovereign Digital Reserves Platform for Governments</title>

Meta description example:

<meta name="description" content="Trupasa helps governments generate income with non-inflationary digital reserves backed by U.S. Treasury Bills – funding public projects with zero inflation.">

Keep it around 150 characters.

URL Structure: If implementing multi-page:

Homepage: https://trupasa.com/ (English default).

Language versions: maybe https://trupasa.com/sv for Swedish, /so for Somali, or use subdomains (sv.trupasa.com) if preferred.

Blog posts: https://trupasa.com/insights/post-title etc. Use SEO-friendly URLs (lowercase, hyphen-separated).

Heading Structure: Only one <h1> (likely the hero headline). Use <h2> for each section title (How It Works, Architecture, etc.). Within sections, if needed, use <h3> for subheadings (like individual FAQ questions could be <h3> if not using list format).

Load Speed: Aim for high PageSpeed scores. Use techniques like lazy-loading images (especially blog thumbnails or any image not immediately in view).

Analytics Setup: Place the GA tracking code (Google Analytics 4 recommended) right before the closing </head> or use Google Tag Manager if in scope. E.g.:

<!-- Global site tag (gtag.js) - Google Analytics -->

<script async src="https://www.googletagmanager.com/gtag/js?id=G-XXXXXXX"></script>

&nbsp; gtag('js', new Date());

&nbsp; gtag('config', 'G-XXXXXXX');

</script>

(Replace G-XXXXXXX with actual GA property ID.)

Social Sharing Preview: Ensure the Open Graph image is appealing. Possibly create a composite image (e.g., Trupasa logo + tagline on a nice background) specifically for sharing. Size ~1200x630 px.

Favicons: Provide a favicon for browsers (16x16 and 32x32 .ico or .png), and perhaps an Apple touch icon, to polish the SEO/branding.

Sitemap and Robots: Provide a sitemap.xml listing at least the homepage and blog posts. robots.txt should allow all (or disallow sensitive pages if any).

International SEO: If multiple languages are served, use hreflang tags in the HTML head to indicate alternate language versions (e.g., hreflang="en", "sv", "so" for respective URLs). This helps Google serve the right language to the right users.

Structured Data: Optionally, implement Organization schema:

<script type="application/ld+json">

}

</script>

And perhaps Article schema for each blog post page (when those exist).

Testing: Before launch, test the page with SEO tools or validators (Google’s Rich Results Test, Mobile-Friendly test, etc.) to catch any issues.

By adhering to these SEO and analytics guidelines, the Trupasa landing page will not only communicate well to its human audience but also be well-understood by search engines and ready for digital marketing efforts.
